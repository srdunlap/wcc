Dian Fossey

When you realize the value of all life, you dwell less on what is past and concentrate more on the preservation of the future.

—Dian Fossey

Dian Fossey was an American zoologist, primatologist, and anthropologist who undertook an extensive study of mountain gorilla groups over a period of 18 years. She studied them daily in the mountain forests of Rwanda, initially encouraged to work there by anthropologist Louis Leakey. Her 1983 book, Gorillas in the Mist, combines her scientific study of the gorillas at Karisoke Research Center with her own personal story. It was adapted into a 1988 film of the same name. Fossey was murdered in 1985; the case remains open.

Interesting Facts

  1. Jane Goodall, Birute Galdikas, and Dian Fossey were called the "Trimates"—a group of prominent primatologists.
  2. Dian Fossey realized her love of animals at 19, on a farm in Montana.
  3. Fossey initially had difficulties with basic sciences in school.
  4. Fossey strongly opposed wildlife tourism because gorillas can catch human diseases.
